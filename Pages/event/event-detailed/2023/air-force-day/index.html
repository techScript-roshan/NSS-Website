Awareness on indian Air Force Day

About the event

The National Service Scheme (NSS) successfully orchestrated a noteworthy one-day event, the "Indian Air Force Day Celebration," on October 9, 2023. Aimed at fostering peer-to-peer learning, this offline program delved into the intricacies of the Indian Defence Forces. Facilitated by distinguished individuals, including Dr. Amrinder Singh Pannu and Prof. Jasveer Singh, the event featured Group Captain B.S Gill as the esteemed Chief Guest. The event's objectives encompassed offering participants a glimpse of the Indian Air Force, showcasing its achievements, exploring joining opportunities, and elucidating the scope of engineering within this domain.
The inauguration witnessed the escort of Group Captain B.S. Gill by NCC Cadets of the Army Wing and Air Wing, followed by a warm welcome from the spectators. The comprehensive presentation by Group Captain B.S Gill outlined various pathways to join the Indian Defence Forces, spanning Army, Navy, and Air Force entries. These included NDA & NA Examination, 10+2 TES, CDS Examination, TGC Entry, SSC Tech Entry, NCC Special Entry, JAG Entry for the Army; 10+2 TES, NAIC, Pilot & Observer Entry, and Executive & Technical Branch Entries for the Navy; and FCAT Examination and Meteorology Department for the Air Force.
A pivotal aspect of the event was a short movie portraying the role of modern technology in the Indian Air Force and Defence Forces, accentuating their continuous evolution. An interactive session facilitated by Group Captain B.S Gill allowed students to pose questions and gain insights, effectively addressing their doubts regarding the Indian Defence Forces.
The event culminated with the honoring of Group Captain B.S Gill by the present faculties of NCC cadets and NSS members, marking the success of an informative and engaging initiative that provided participants with a comprehensive understanding of the Indian Air Force and Defence Forces, emphasizing career opportunities and the role of engineering in this esteemed sector.
